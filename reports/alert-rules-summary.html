● MA Bounce — SMA Support

MA Bounce 20
BUY Enabled
Price pulls back to the 20-day SMA in an uptrend and bounces above it.
Why it triggers: The 20MA is the most responsive institutional moving average. In an uptrend (20MA > 50MA), pullbacks to the 20MA attract dip buyers and algorithms. A close back above confirms the pullback was healthy, not a trend reversal. This is the highest-frequency bounce signal.
Uptrend confirmed: SMA20 > SMA50
Bar low within 0.3% of SMA20
Bar closes above SMA20 (bounce confirmed)
Stop: 0.5% below SMA20
Example: NVDA at $183 — SMA20=$180, bar wicks to $179.60 (0.2% from SMA20), closes $181 → BUY signal, stop $179.10, T1 $182.90
MA Bounce 50
BUY Enabled
Deeper pullback to the 50-day SMA and bounce. Counter-trend bounces allowed with reduced confidence.
Why it triggers: The 50MA is a key institutional level — pension funds and large caps often defend this zone. A bounce here after a multi-day pullback is a higher-conviction entry than the 20MA because the discount is larger. Counter-trend bounces (price was below 50MA) fire at "medium" confidence because they're riskier but can catch V-bottoms.
Bar low within 0.3% of SMA50
Bar closes above SMA50
Counter-trend: prior close ≤ SMA50 → medium confidence
Stop: 0.5% below SMA50
Example: PLTR pulls from $160 to $153 over 5 days — SMA50=$152.98, bar low $152.60, closes $154 → BUY, medium confidence (counter-trend), stop $152.22
MA Bounce 100
BUY Enabled
Bounce at the 100-day SMA — intermediate institutional level with wider proximity tolerance.
Why it triggers: The 100MA sits between the fast (20/50) and slow (200) averages. It's heavily watched by swing traders and intermediate-term funds. The wider 0.5% proximity accounts for deeper wicks common at this level. Bounces here during broad market corrections often mark higher lows.
Bar low within 0.5% of SMA100
Bar closes above SMA100
Stop: 0.7% below SMA100 (wider)
MA Bounce 200
BUY Enabled
Bounce at the 200-day SMA — the most widely watched institutional level.
Why it triggers: The 200MA defines the long-term trend for virtually every institutional investor. When SPY or a mega-cap bounces here, it's often a "line in the sand" moment — massive buy programs activate. The widest proximity (0.8%) and stop (1.0%) reflect the deep wicks these levels produce before the reversal sticks.
Bar low within 0.8% of SMA200
Bar closes above SMA200
Stop: 1.0% below SMA200 (widest)

● EMA Bounce — Exponential MA Support

EMA Bounce 20
BUY Enabled
Price pulls back to the 20-day EMA in an uptrend and bounces. Mirrors MA Bounce 20 but uses exponential weighting.
Why it triggers: EMAs react faster to price changes than SMAs. In V-recoveries, the EMA can be significantly below the SMA (e.g., PLTR EMA50=$153 vs SMA50=$159 — a $6 gap). The EMA catches the bounce signal days earlier because it weights recent prices more heavily. Running both SMA and EMA bounces ensures no signals are missed during rapid reversals.
Uptrend: EMA20 > EMA50
Bar low within 0.3% of EMA20
Bar closes above EMA20
Stop: 0.5% below EMA20
Example: After a sell-off, PLTR EMA20=$148 while SMA20=$152 — price touches $147.80, closes $149 → EMA Bounce fires 2 days before the SMA bounce would have triggered
EMA Bounce 50
BUY Enabled
Deeper pullback to the 50-day EMA and bounce. Counter-trend allowed.
Why it triggers: Same logic as SMA Bounce 50 but with EMA's faster responsiveness. Particularly valuable during sharp V-bottoms where EMA50 catches up to falling prices faster, providing an earlier entry signal. Counter-trend bounces (price was below EMA50) included to catch mean-reversion plays.
Bar low within 0.3% of EMA50
Bar closes above EMA50
Counter-trend allowed (reduced confidence)

● Support Reclaim & Reversal

Prior Day Low Reclaim
BUY Enabled
Price dips below yesterday's low, then recovers above it — bullish reversal / bear trap.
Why it triggers: Prior day low is a key S/R level visible to every trader. A dip below triggers stop-losses and short entries. When price reclaims back above, those shorts are trapped and must cover — creating a short squeeze effect. Gap-down days get boosted confidence because the gap fill provides additional upside fuel.
Some bar dipped below PDL by ≥ 0.1%
Last bar closes back above prior day low
Gap down day → boosted to high confidence
Session Low Double Bottom
BUY Enabled
Session low tested twice with meaningful recovery between — classic intraday double bottom.
Why it triggers: A double bottom at the session low is the most reliable intraday reversal pattern. The first test establishes the level, the recovery proves buyers exist, and the second test (on declining volume) shows sellers are exhausted. The volume cap (< 1.2x avg) on the retest is critical — if volume is high, it's panic selling, not exhaustion.
First touch ≥ 30 min ago
Recovery: 4+ bars above session low + 0.3%
Retest volume < 1.2x average (exhaustion)
Last bar closes above session low
Intraday Support Bounce
BUY Enabled
Price bounces off a confirmed intraday support level (hourly lows that held).
Why it triggers: Intraday supports are detected by finding hourly lows where the next hour's low stayed above and price bounced. The lookback window (last 6 bars / 30 min) catches bounces that started a few bars ago. A staleness guard (max 1% above support) prevents firing on stale signals where price already ran.
Scan last 6 bars for support touch
Touch within 0.3% of support level
Price hasn't run > 1.0% above support
Volume ≥ 0.4x average (not dead tape)
VWAP Reclaim
BUY Enabled
V-shape morning reversal — session low in first hour, price recovers through VWAP on volume.
Why it triggers: This is one of the highest-conviction intraday patterns. Institutional traders use VWAP as their benchmark — when price flushes below VWAP in the morning (triggering retail panic) and then reclaims above it on rising volume, it signals that institutional buying has overwhelmed the morning sellers. The morning timing requirement ensures this is a genuine reversal, not a random VWAP cross.
Session low in first 60 min
≥ 15 min elapsed since session low
Last bar closes above VWAP
Recovery ≥ 0.5% from session low
Volume ≥ 1.2x average

● Breakout & Momentum

Prior Day High Breakout
BUY Enabled
Price breaks above yesterday's high with volume confirmation — bullish continuation.
Why it triggers: The prior day high is the most visible resistance level. A close above it on elevated volume signals that sellers at that level have been absorbed and buyers have control. The volume requirement (1.2x avg) filters out low-conviction pokes that are likely to fail.
Close above prior day high
Volume ≥ 1.2x average
Stop = last bar low (risk-capped)
Weekly High Breakout
BUY Enabled
Price breaks above prior week's high with volume — higher-timeframe breakout.
Why it triggers: The prior week high represents a multi-day resistance level. Breaking above it signals that the stock is entering a new weekly range, which attracts momentum traders and trend-following algorithms. The weekly range is used for target calculation — T2 targets the full weekly measured move.
Close above prior week high
Volume ≥ 1.2x average
T2 = entry + prior week range
Outside Day Breakout
BUY Enabled
Follow-through breakout from a bullish outside day — continuation above yesterday's range engulfment.
Why it triggers: An outside day (range engulfs prior day) that closes in the upper portion signals a power shift from sellers to buyers. When the next day breaks above that high, it confirms the trend change. Stop is placed at the midpoint of the outside day range — if price falls back that far, the pattern has failed.
Prior day was bullish outside day
Current bar closes above prior day high
Stop = midpoint of outside day range
EMA Crossover 5/20
BUY Enabled
Daily EMA5 crosses above EMA20 — bullish momentum shift on the daily timeframe.
Why it triggers: The 5/20 EMA crossover on daily bars is a classic momentum signal used by swing traders. It catches the transition from pullback to new uptrend. The widened 3-day detection window ensures crossovers that happen on a Friday close aren't missed by Monday. Anti-flicker filter (0.05% separation) prevents whipsaw signals. Now fires for all symbols — no longer restricted to mega-caps.
EMA5 crosses above EMA20 on daily bars
Scans last 3 days for crossover
Separation ≥ 0.05% (anti-flicker)
Crossover day candle must be green
EMA5 still above EMA20 today
Example: PLTR EMA5 crossed above EMA20 on 03-03 (Friday) — detected on Monday because of the 3-day lookback window

● Planned & Weekly Level Touch

Planned Level Touch
BUY Enabled
Price touches the daily Scanner's pre-planned entry or support levels and bounces.
Why it triggers: The Scanner computes optimal entry/support levels each day based on daily patterns. When intraday price touches these pre-planned levels, it fires the alert. This is the "plan your trade, trade your plan" rule — you defined the level in the morning and price has arrived. Uses wider 0.5% proximity to cover the full buy zone.
Daily plan exists for symbol
Bar low within 0.5% of plan entry/support
Bar close ≥ that level (hold confirmed)
Weekly Level Touch
BUY Enabled
Price touches prior week's low and bounces — institutional support level.
Why it triggers: The prior week low is a major structural level visible on weekly charts. Institutional traders often accumulate at weekly lows. T1 targets the prior week high (full weekly range recovery), making the reward profile very attractive when the entry works.
Bar low within 0.4% of prior week low
Bar closes above prior week low
T1 = prior week high
Stop: 0.5% below prior week low

● Resistance & Warnings

Resistance at Prior High
NOTICE Enabled
Price reaches prior day's high — take profits or watch for rejection.
Why it triggers: The prior day high is where sellers stepped in yesterday. Price reaching this level often triggers profit-taking and new short entries. If you have an active position, this is your cue to tighten stops or take partial profits. Without a position, it's an early warning of resistance ahead.
Bar high within 0.3% of prior day high
Weekly High Resistance
SELL Enabled
Price approaches prior week's high from below and gets rejected.
Why it triggers: The prior week high is a major multi-day resistance level. A rejection here (high wicks into the level but close stays below) means sellers are defending it. If you're long, this is a strong signal to take profits. Distinct from weekly high breakout — the breakout fires when price closes above the level.
Bar high within 0.4% of prior week high
Bar close < prior week high (rejection)
EMA Resistance
SELL Enabled
Price rallies into overhead EMA20 or EMA50 and gets rejected — bearish warning.
Why it triggers: When price is below an EMA, that EMA acts as dynamic resistance. Rallies that wick into the EMA but close below it indicate sellers are using the EMA as a reference to add shorts. Especially important when the EMA was recently broken — "role flip" from support to resistance. The EMA moves faster than the SMA, so this catches rejections earlier.
Bar high within 0.3% of EMA20 or EMA50
Bar close < EMA (rejection confirmed)
Role-flip annotation if prior close was below EMA
MA Resistance
SELL Enabled
Price rallies into overhead SMA (20/50/100/200) and gets rejected.
Why it triggers: Overhead SMAs act as ceilings during downtrends. The rule scans MAs from lowest to highest and fires for the first rejecting MA — the most immediately relevant resistance. Combined with EMA Resistance, this provides comprehensive moving average overhead detection for both SMA and EMA traders.
Scans SMA 20 → 50 → 100 → 200 (nearest first)
Bar high within 0.3% of MA
Bar close below MA (rejection)
Hourly Resistance Approach
SELL Enabled
Active trade approaching hourly swing high resistance — tighten or take profits.
Why it triggers: Only fires when you have an active position. Hourly swing highs from multi-day 1-hour bars represent tested intraday resistance zones. When your position is approaching one, this alert gives you advance warning to lock in profits before price potentially reverses.
Active entry exists
Bar high within 0.3% of hourly swing high
Resistance at Prior Low
NOTICE Enabled
Price rallies up to prior day's low from below and gets rejected — role flip.
Why it triggers: When price breaks below the prior day low and then tries to rally back, the broken support often acts as resistance (role flip). Rejection here signals that the downtrend is intact and lower prices are likely. Important context for avoiding false "recovery" signals.
Bar high within 0.3% of prior day low
Bar close below prior day low (rejection)

● Trade Management

Target 1 Hit
SELL Enabled
Price reaches Target 1 (1R) — sell half, move stop to breakeven.
Why it triggers: At 1R you've achieved a 1:1 reward-to-risk ratio. Selling half locks in profit and moving the stop to breakeven makes the remaining position a "free trade." This is the core trade management discipline that turns winning trades into risk-free runners.
Bar high ≥ target 1 price (uses session-wide high)
Target 2 Hit
SELL Enabled
Price reaches Target 2 (2R) — take remaining position off.
Why it triggers: At 2R the trade has achieved a 2:1 reward-to-risk — an excellent outcome. Taking the remaining position off at this level maximizes realised P&L. Targets are set using smart resistance levels (prior highs, MAs, weekly levels) when available, falling back to R-multiples.
Bar high ≥ target 2 price (uses session-wide high)
Stop Loss Hit
SELL Enabled
Price hits stop loss level from an active entry — exit immediately.
Why it triggers: Discipline. When the stop is hit, the thesis is invalidated. The alert uses session-wide low (not just the last bar) to catch stops that were breached between poll intervals. No excuses, no hoping — exit and preserve capital.
Bar low ≤ stop price (uses session-wide low)
Auto Stop-Out
SELL Enabled
Prior BUY entry's stop breached — automated exit signal, triggers cooldown.
Why it triggers: Tracks every BUY signal's entry/stop pair. When the stop is breached, it fires an immediate exit signal and starts a 30 min cooldown (COOLDOWN_MINUTES) that suppresses new BUY signals. This prevents revenge trading after a stop-out — the most common cause of compounding losses.
Tracked BUY entry exists with valid stop
Bar low ≤ stop price
Triggers 30 min BUY cooldown

● Exit — Support Breakdown

Support Breakdown
SHORT Enabled
Support broken with high volume and conviction close — exit long immediately.
Why it triggers: When prior day low or intraday support breaks on 1.5x volume with a conviction close (in the lower 30% of bar range), the structure has changed. This is not a short entry signal — it's an emergency exit for any long position. The conviction close requirement filters out momentary wicks. When it fires, all BUY signals for the same symbol are suppressed (breakdown day filter). Session low breaks get boosted to high confidence.
Close below min(prior day low, nearest support)
Volume ≥ 1.5x average
Close in lower 30% of bar range
Exit-only: requires active position to fire
Suppresses all BUY signals for this symbol

● Post-Signal Filters

After rules fire, signals pass through these filters before delivery. Filters can suppress, demote, or enrich signals.

Dedup (fired_today)

Each (symbol, alert_type) pair fires once per session. Prevents alert fatigue from repeated 5-min bar triggers.

Noise Filter

BUY signals on very low volume (< 0.4x avg) are dropped — likely noise on a dead tape.

Staleness Filter

BUY signals where price already ran past entry + 1R are dropped — the trade is no longer at a good risk/reward.

Overhead MA Resistance

BUY signals are suppressed when an SMA sits within 0.5% above the entry — buying into immediate resistance.

Breakdown Day Suppression

When a Support Breakdown fires, all BUY signals for that symbol are removed — the structure has changed.

Cooldown (30 min)

After an Auto Stop-Out, all BUY signals are suppressed for 30 minutes to prevent revenge trading.

SPY Regime Demotion

BUY confidence demoted in CHOPPY or TRENDING_DOWN regimes. SPY at resistance also demotes; SPY at support can boost.

Relative Strength

If symbol is falling 2x+ harder than SPY intraday, BUY confidence is demoted — it's underperforming the market.

RSI Adjustments

SPY RSI < 35 (oversold) can boost BUY; SPY RSI > 70 (overbought) demotes. Per-symbol RSI < 35 flags crash risk.

MA Confluence Boost

When a horizontal support entry aligns with a nearby MA (within 0.5%), confidence is boosted and flagged as "confluence."

Volume Exhaustion

Seller exhaustion (declining vol on pullback) boosts BUY confidence. Buyer exhaustion (volume climax) adds caution.

Score & Consolidation

Each signal scored 0-100 (A+/A/B/C). Multiple BUY signals consolidate — best score wins, boosted by confirming signals.

Intraday Stage Trading

PDH / PDL + VWAP slope — a study guide for tomorrow's session

The core idea: price action at PDH/PDL only matters in context. The stage tells you which signals to take and which to skip. Right signal in the wrong stage = a losing trade. Right signal in the right stage = your edge.

The Mental Model

Three pieces define every stage:

  1. Position — where price sits relative to PDH and PDL (above / inside / below)
  2. VWAP slope — rising / flat / falling over the last 20 bars
  3. Price vs VWAP — above or below

Combine these three and you get the stage. The badge does this automatically — your job is to read the badge and obey.

The 6 States

Stage 2 — Advancing

Trend Long

close > PDH
AND VWAP rising (> +0.1%)
AND price > VWAP
  • Take BREAK+ longs above PDH
  • Take RECLAIM longs at PDH
  • Take SWEEP+ diamonds (any stage)
  • Buy pullbacks to PDH or VWAP
  • Don't short — fighting trend
  • Don't take REJECT triangles
Example: TSLA broke PDH 376 with rising VWAP, ran to 382. The pullback to PDH = buy zone. Stop below VWAP.
Stage 4 — Declining

Trend Short

close < PDL
AND VWAP falling (< -0.1%)
AND price < VWAP
  • Take BREAK- shorts below PDL
  • Take REJECT shorts at PDL
  • Take SWEEP- diamonds (any stage)
  • Short pops to VWAP (acts as resistance)
  • Don't long — fighting trend
  • Don't take RECLAIM triangles
Example: NVDA cliff candle dumped 210 to 200. VWAP rolling. Each bounce to VWAP 201.50 = continuation short.
Stage 3 — Topping

Distribution / Caution

close > PDH
AND VWAP flat OR falling
(Stage 2 momentum has died)
  • Trim or exit longs
  • Take REJECT shorts at PDH (counter-trend fade)
  • Take SWEEP- diamonds aggressively
  • No new longs — risk of trap
  • Don't take BREAK+ — likely fade
Example: Price held above PDH for 4-5 bars but VWAP went flat then rolled. This is the warning shot before Stage 4 flips on.
Stage 1 — Basing

Chop / No Trade

close inside PDH–PDL range
AND VWAP flat
  • Wait for stage flip or sweep
  • Take SWEEP+/SWEEP- diamonds (high conviction)
  • Don't trade BREAK+ / BREAK- — likely fail
  • Don't trade RECLAIM / REJECT in chop
  • No size pressure — flat is fine
Example: SPY chopping between PDH and PDL with VWAP flat. 90% of breakouts here fake out. Sit on hands.
Transitioning

No Clean Regime

Doesn't fit Stage 1/2/3/4 cleanly
e.g. above PDH but price < VWAP
e.g. below PDL but VWAP rising
  • HALF SIZE if any trade
  • Take SWEEP diamonds only
  • Wait for stage to clarify
  • Don't take ANY triangle signals
  • Don't force a bias
Example: Price below PDL but VWAP rising = potential bottoming. Could resolve up (reclaim) or down (continuation). Wait.
Insufficient Data

Pre-Open / Loading

VWAP slope not yet calculable
(first 20 bars of session)
  • Wait for slope to populate (~20 bars)
  • Don't trade the first 15-20 minutes
  • Don't trust the badge yet
Example: First hour of session on a 65m chart = badge will read INSUFFICIENT DATA. Watch open, don't trade.

SWEEP vs BREAK / REJECT

Triangles fire on every PDH/PDL event — most fail in chop. Diamonds (sweeps) are A+ reversal setups that work in any stage.

◆ SWEEP+ — Long Reversal

Pattern: price breaks BELOW PDL, triggers stops, reverses back ABOVE PDL within 5 bars.

  1. Bar closes below PDL — short stops trigger, late shorts pile in
  2. Smart money fills longs against the panic
  3. Price reclaims back above PDL within 5 bars
  4. Trapped shorts cover = fuel for the rally

Trade: Long on the reclaim. Stop below the sweep wick. Target = PDH.

◆ SWEEP- — Short Reversal

Pattern: price breaks ABOVE PDH, triggers stops, fails back BELOW PDH within 5 bars.

  1. Bar closes above PDH — buy stops trigger, late longs FOMO in
  2. Smart money fills shorts against the panic
  3. Price drops back below PDH within 5 bars
  4. Trapped longs sell = fuel for the dump

Trade: Short on the failure. Stop above the sweep wick. Target = PDL.

Mental rule: "Did the level break and hold? Trend trade (BREAK / RECLAIM). Did the level break and fail? Reversal trade (SWEEP — much higher conviction)." When in doubt, wait for a diamond.

Decision Flow at Every Triangle

When a triangle fires on the chart: 1. Read the badge. | v 2. Is the signal direction stage-aligned? | YES | NO | | v v 3a. Take it. 3b. Skip it. | v 4. Manage with stage-aware stops: Stage 2 long -> stop below VWAP or PDH Stage 4 short -> stop above VWAP or PDL Sweep -> stop just past the wick When a diamond fires: ALWAYS take it (regardless of stage). Sweeps are universal A+ setups.

Daily Routine

  • Open chart on 65m timeframe (or 1h for slower stocks). Avoid 5/15m — VWAP slope too noisy.
  • First 15 minutes: do not trade. Badge reads INSUFFICIENT DATA until ~20 bars print.
  • Once badge populates: read stage, set bias for the session.
  • Build a small watchlist (5-7 names) — don't try to trade 20 charts.
  • Set TradingView alerts on each name — Telegram routes JSON with stage tag.
  • Position sizing: stage-aligned signal = full size. Sweep = full size. TRANSITIONING = half size or skip. Counter-trend = zero.
  • If no diamond fires AND stage stays mixed all day: zero trades is the right answer. Cash is a position.
  • End of session: log every trade against the badge state at entry. Review weekly — count which stages produced winners.

Three Ground Truths

  1. Sweeps work in any stage. Diamonds always trade — they're the failed-breakout reversal pattern, market-condition agnostic.
  2. Triangles only work in their matched stage. A BREAK+ in Stage 1 is a coin flip. A BREAK+ in Stage 2 is your edge.
  3. The badge protects you from yourself. Most blown-up trader days come from forcing trades in chop. The "sweeps only" rule is the discipline you didn't know you needed.
One sentence to remember tomorrow: "If the badge doesn't agree with my trade direction, I don't take the trade — unless it's a diamond."
